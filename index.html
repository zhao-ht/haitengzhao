Bio

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Machine Intelligence at Peking University. Currently, I am conducting research at Sigma Lab, Peking University, supervised by Prof. Zhihong Deng. I am also working at the University of Hong Kong with Lingpeng Kong and Qi Liu on many exciting projects.

Previously, I attained a B.Sc. degree in Psychology from Peking University. My current focus lies in exploring whether deep learning can effectively encode human-like intelligence, encompassing transfer, generalization, reasoning and planning capacities, and more.

As a researcher, I am focused on exploring various aspects related to the generalization of deep models. This involves investigating domains such as domain adaptation, transfer learning, and robustness, among several other fascinating questions. Furthermore, I am particularly interested in investigating how deep models can effectively handle structured problems, which include understanding structured data and knowledge, structured and symbolic problem-solving, compositional generalization, and much more. I anticipate chatting or collaborating with you!
