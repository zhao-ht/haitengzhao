Bio

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Machine Intelligence at Peking University. Currently, I am conducting research at Sigma Lab, Peking University, supervised by Prof. Zhihong Deng. I am also working at the University of Hong Kong with Lingpeng Kong and Qi Liu on many exciting projects.

Previously, I attained a B.Sc. degree in Psychology from Peking University. My current focus lies in exploring whether deep learning can effectively encode human-like intelligence, encompassing transfer, generalization, reasoning and planning capacities, and more.

As a researcher, I focus on various aspects of deep learning. My work primarily spans three key areas:

  1. Machine Learning Theory: Investigating topics such as generalization theory, domain adaptation, transfer learning, and robustness.
  2. Structured Data Understanding: Exploring how deep models can effectively handle structured problems, including understanding structured data and knowledge, solving symbolic problems, and more. My current research in this area emphasizes AI for Science (AI4Science).
  3. Reasoning and Planning: Addressing compositional generalization challenges in deep models, which are crucial for achieving human-like intelligence.

I look forward to chatting and collaborating with you!
